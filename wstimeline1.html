TIME-LINE

How it all went on and on.

TIME-LINE COVID-19

Dec 1, 2019 till Jan 17, 2020 :

First Cases Reported in China; Outbreak Spreads to Other Countries

Week of Jan 18 till Jan 24:

First Cases Appear in United States and Europe; The Closing of Wuhan

Week of Jan 25 till Jan 31:

First Cases Appear in Many More Countries; Travel Bans Spread

Week of Feb 1 till Feb 7:

First Deaths Outside China; Coordinated G7 Response; Chinese Stock Markets Plunge

Week of Feb 8 till Feb 14:

Death Toll Surpasses That of SARS; WHO-led Team Heads for China; Cruise Ship Cases Surpass 100

Week of Feb 15 till Feb 21:

Cruise Ship Cases Rise; Outbreaks in Iran, Italy, and South Korea; More Deaths Outside Mainland China

Week of Feb 22 till Feb 28:

Outbreaks Grow in Iran, Italy, and South Korea; Travel Restrictions Increase; Middle East and Europe Grapple With Spread

Week of Feb 29 till March 6:

New Outbreaks Increase in Europe and the Americas; More Deaths Outside China; SXSW Festival Cancelled.

Week of March 7 till March 13:

One Fifth of All Students Worldwide are out of School; New York City Declares State of Emergency; NBA Cancels Season

Week of March 14 till March 21:

China reports no new Coronavirus cases for its Third Consecutive Day; Italy becomes the country with the highest death-toll; President Trump signs the Families First Coronavirus Response Act into Law

Week of March 22 till March 29:

Nearly one third of the world's population is living under coronavirus-related restrictions; Japan Postpones 2020 Summer Olympics; the WHO warns there is a "significant shortage" of medical supplies

Week of March 30 till April 4:

Worldwide coronavirus cases exceed one million; over 10 million Americans file for unemployment; Wimbledon Tennis Tournament canceled for the first time since World War Two

Week of April 5 till April 12:

Wet markets reopen in Wuhan; the United States has the most Coronavirus deaths in the World; Boris Johnson Discharged from Intensive Care

Week of April 13 till April 20:

China reports its first economic contraction in a decade; Austria becomes one of the first European countries to Reopen; President Trump Suspends Immigration

Week of April 21 till April 28:

President Trump suspends immigration; China pledges $30 million to the WHO; Italy and Spain ease lockdown restrictions
